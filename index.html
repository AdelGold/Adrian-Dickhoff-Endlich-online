Rotis Film

Interview with Vinca Kruk in collaboration with Timothee Charon outside Otl Aichers former Studio in Rotis. The Font is a monospaced version of the Rotis Font. The Interview's layout is set like a screenplay relating to her work as a directress and her noting like it felt she was now in our constructed reality.



Format: 120mm x 190mm
Questions
by Sereina Rothenberger and David Bennewith
2017/18, HfG Karlsruhe

Xasthur

Editorial about the californian musician Scott Conner alias Malefic formerly known as Xasthur .
The Layout is dark and heavy, much like Scott Connors music and his lifestyle as presented by himself. The Interviews are black on white and lighter in tone. They demystify and ground the man behind the extreme music.



Format: 175mm x 270mm
Typographie II
under Philippe Karrer
2015 HfG Karlsruhe
The Adventures of Cunibert and Reginald

A comic about an insecure Knight, drawn in thick black lines and his silent compagnion Reginald the horse. Trust into a complex World he doesn't understand and doesn't try to, longing to find meaning or purpose in a World that is seemingly against him. The World is detaile and drawn with a thinner line to emphasize Cuniberts feeling of not belonging.




Format: 202mm x 281mm
Worldbuilding Step One
by Marie Pohl
2019 HfG Karlsruhe

Gestalten des Rechts

In celebration of the 70th birthday of Germanys Constitution the talkshow "Im Namen des Volkes Deutschland fragt zum Grundgesetz" was filmed in the HfG. We were tasked with producing a 70 second long clip visualizing parts of the Constitution. We used principles of reduction, abstraction and simplification to visualize a slection of laws.



Cooperation with the ARD, ZDF and SWR
Support from Prof. Sereina Rothenberger and Anton Stuckardt
In Collaborattion with Tatjana Pfeiffer
2019

Headlights

The inspiration for the font was the image of a speeding car aproaching during darkness blinding the viewer as it gets closer. Each letter of the font has seven cuts. These seven cuts alternate in order as one writes, creating the feeling of one car after the other going by.



Format: 175mm x 270mm
Realist Typography
by Jung Myung Lee
2017/18 HfG Karlsruhe

This is Europe

In trying to better understand and showcase why and how Brexit could have happend I compiled news headlines, campaign slogans, statements from the debates and poll results leading up to the vote. Underlined by comments from Social media where a major part of the debate took place.
The zine is part of a series made by Students of the HfG which are part of the Special Edition of the "Slanted Magazine #34Europe".



Format: 130mm x 186mm
This is Europe
by Julia Kahl
2019, HfG Karlsruhe
